# Legal Aid Channel Guide

## Overview

Prepare concise consultation materials and channel-selection notes for labor-dispute help. Eligibility, materials, and handling routes must be verified with local official channels.

## When to Use

- The user plans to contact a legal-aid center, public legal service center, labor union, labor inspection department, labor arbitration commission, 12348, or 12333.
- The user needs a one-page summary, evidence index, claim draft, or consultation question list.
- Local service channels or filing requirements are uncertain.

## Output

- One-page case summary.
- Evidence index.
- Draft claims and estimated amounts with assumptions.
- Questions to ask the service provider.
- Local channel lookup prompt including province/city, official portal, search keywords, and last-reviewed date.

## Guardrails

- State that legal-aid eligibility is reviewed by the local agency.
- Do not claim a specific office will accept the case unless verified by current official information.
- Remind the user to call or check 12348, 12333, local human-resources/social-security departments, judicial administration departments, labor inspection, or arbitration commission channels for urgent limitation-period and filing questions.
